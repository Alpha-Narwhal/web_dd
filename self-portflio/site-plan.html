Home page

**Introduction**: Welcome to my professional portfolio! I'm Matthew Owen, a cybersecurity student at BYU-Idaho. I am passionate about exploring various aspects of cybersecurity and aim to contribute to secure digital environments. Explore my site to learn more about my skills, experiences, and projects that showcase my journey in the field.

**Career Goals**: As a sophomore in cybersecurity at BYU-Idaho, my immediate goal is to deepen my understanding and practical knowledge in the field. I aim to secure internships and part-time opportunities that will allow me to apply my academic learning to real-world challenges. In the long term, I aspire to enter the cybersecurity industry, with a particular interest in exploring various roles. While I'm still discovering the specific areas that intrigue me, I am drawn to becoming a red teamer and engaging in penetration testing. However, given the vastness of the industry, I remain open to other opportunities that align with my evolving interests and skills.

**Professional Skills**: - **Hands-On Learning**: One of my greatest strengths is learning by jumping in and interacting with new things, figuring them out by using and working with them. - **Learning New Things**: In regards to tech, this means figuring out how to get something done as well as troubleshooting as I go, even with tasks I've never done before. - **Learning from Others**: I have learned to effectively work with others around me to expand my understanding and knowledge without wasting too much of their time. - **Broad Perspective**: I maintain an awareness of how my role and contributions fit into the overall objectives and success of projects and teams. - **Communication**: Ability to clearly and concisely convey technical information. - **Team Collaboration**: Experience in working effectively within team environments. - **Problem-Solving**: Strong analytical skills to diagnose issues and develop effective solutions. - **Time Management**: Proven ability to manage multiple tasks and deadlines efficiently.

**Site Overview**: Explore my site to find detailed information on the following sections: - **Resume**: On this page, you'll find a link to my detailed resume, outlining my educational background, relevant coursework, and professional experiences. Additionally, I provide a deeper explanation of my journey in cybersecurity, highlighting significant milestones and learning experiences. - **Portfolio**: This section showcases various projects I've worked on, providing descriptions and links to each project. You can explore my work, see the challenges I tackled, and understand the skills I utilized to complete these projects.

Portfolio

**Introduction**: Welcome to my portfolio! This page is a living list of projects I've created or been a part of. As I continue to explore and develop my skills in cybersecurity, I'll be adding new projects and updates here. Below, you'll find the details of my first project, "Dangerscape USA."

**Project 1: Dangerscape USA**: "Dangerscape USA" was developed during BYU-Idaho's I-Hack 24-hour hackathon, where I worked with a team of four. This project features a map that displays every county in the USA, assessing various dangers including auto accidents, natural disasters, and violent crime. Each category is broken down into subcategories, providing a comprehensive danger rating for each county, with filter options to customize the data view. **My Contributions**: - **Map API Integration**: Ensured the map API was functional and interactive, allowing seamless integration with our team's database. - **Webpage Design**: Designed the entire webpage that hosts the map, including HTML and CSS design. - **Concept Development**: Assisted the team leader in developing ideas for the danger ratings, importance levels, and formulas for calculating scores. - **Supporting Roles**: Filled in gaps to allow other team members to focus on more complex tasks, ensuring a cohesive and high-quality final product.
